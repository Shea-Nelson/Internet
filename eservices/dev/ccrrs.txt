	<h2>Clerk of Court Revenue Remittance System</h2>
	<div>
		<p>Florida Clerks of Court and County Comptrollers collect documentary stamp tax, nonrecurring intangible personal property tax, and over 151 different fines and fees that 
		are distributed to various agencies. Florida law requires these funds be electronically remitted to the Florida Department of Revenue for distribution. Clerks and 
		comptrollers transfer funds for all state agencies to the Department in a single electronic transaction through the 
		<a title="Link to Clerk of Court Revenue Remittance System - This link will open in a new window" href="https://suntax.floridarevenue.com/" target="_blank">Clerk of Court 
		Revenue Remittance System</a>.</p>

		<h3>Remittances Due Weekly</h3>		

			<p>Remittances for documentary stamp tax and nonrecurring intangible personal property tax are due on a weekly  basis. The municipality or county officer must complete 
			the online transaction before 5:00 p.m., ET, on the sixth working day following the close of the week in which the funds were received.</p>

		<h3>Remittances Due Monthly</h3>

			<p>Remittances for related fees, services charges, court costs, and fines are due on a monthly basis. The Clerk must complete the online transaction before 5:00 p.m., ET, 
			on the last working day before the 10th day of the month immediately following the month in which the funds were collected.</p>
		
		<p>For a list of payment due dates, review the Department's <span style="font-style: italic;">Florida e-Services Program Calendar of Remittance Dates for Municipalities and 
		County Officers</span> (<a title="Link to Form DR-659C - This link will open in a new window" href="/Forms_library/current/dr659c.pdf" target="_blank">Form DR-659C 
		<img alt="PDF Icon" src="/PublishingImages/pdficon_small.png"/></a>).</p>

			<h3 id="remit">Electronic File and Pay</h3>
			<div>
				<ul>
					<li><p>Agency worksheets: To assist in preparing your remittance submission, you may want to print blank copies of each agency page to use as a worksheet. To do 
					so, go to each agency page and click the "Print this page" link at the top of the page. You will then have an original set of worksheets that can be photocopied for 
					future use.</p></li>
					
					<li><p>Records: Once a filing is submitted, it is stored for review in the Remittance History area until the detail is posted and the payment is fully processed 
					between financial institutions.</p></li>
					
					<li><p>Correcting a previous submission: If you determine a submission has been made in error (i.e., funds entered on incorrect line, incorrect 
					amount of funds entered, etc.), email the Department's <a title="Link to Revenue Accounting Email" 
					href="mailto:RevenueAccounting@floridarevenue.com">Revenue Accounting</a> section, or call 850-617-8586, to facilitate the correction.</p></li>
					
					<li><p>Replacing a failed electronic payment: Log in to the <a title="Link to Clerk of Court Revenue Remittance System - This link will open in a new window" 
					href="https://suntax.floridarevenue.com/" target="_blank">Clerk of Court Revenue Remittance System</a>, then select the "Returned Item" tab. Select the item that failed, 
					then click "Select" to proceed to the payment screen. Select the bank account from which to make the replacement payment, then submit payment. 
					<strong>Note:</strong> You do not need to re-enter line items or the dollar amount.</p></li> 
				</ul>
			</div>

			<h3 id="update">Updating Information</h3>
			<div>
				<ul>
					<li><p>Change clerk's name: If you need to change the name of the clerk, send a request to the Department's Revenue Accounting Section 
					explaining the reason for the change and include official documentation to substantiate the change.</p></li>
					<li><p>Change your password: Log in to the <a title="Link to Clerk of Court Revenue Remittance System - This link will open in a new window" 
					href="https://suntax.floridarevenue.com/" target="_blank">Clerk of Court Revenue Remittance System</a>, then select the "Account Information" tab. Select "Change 
					User Password", enter the required information, then click "Save" for the change to take affect.</p></li>
					<li><p>Contact Information on File and Pay page: Fill in the name, phone number and e-mail address of the person responsible for submitting 
					the revenue remittance. This will be the person the Department contacts if problems occur with the transmission/payment.</p></li>
				</ul>
